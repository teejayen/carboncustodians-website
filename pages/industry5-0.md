## Industry 5.0: Recovering the Missing Link

Industry 5.0 represents a paradigm shift in industrial development—the first industrial evolution ever led by humans. Going beyond Industry 4.0's focus on digitalization, Industry 5.0 recovers the missing link between nature and human-made ecosystems, creating a pathway toward a wasteless world for all.

<div class="quote-block">
  <blockquote>
    "Industry 5.0 is not a sales tool or a buzzword, but a new global ecosystem that helps to reduce the negative impact of industrial development on the global ecosystem."
  </blockquote>
  <cite>— Michael Rada, Founder of Industry 5.0</cite>
</div>

## The Five Waste Categories

At the core of Industry 5.0 is the recognition of five distinct waste categories that can be systematically prevented:

<div class="waste-categories">
  <div class="waste-category">
    <i class="icon icon-physical-waste"></i>
    <h3>Physical Waste</h3>
    <p>Generally known as trash and garbage, kept in waste containers, bins, and landfills.</p>
  </div>
  
  <div class="waste-category">
    <i class="icon icon-social-waste"></i>
    <h3>Social Waste</h3>
    <p>Includes people and animals cut from "meaningful life" or kept for serving the best dead as food stock.</p>
  </div>
  
  <div class="waste-category">
    <i class="icon icon-urban-waste"></i>
    <h3>Urban Waste</h3>
    <p>Comprises empty buildings, factories, flats, and malls all over cities across the globe while millions remain homeless and jobless.</p>
  </div>
  
  <div class="waste-category">
    <i class="icon icon-process-waste"></i>
    <h3>Process Waste</h3>
    <p>Non-efficient or non-effective processes that meaninglessly turn us into components.</p>
  </div>
  
  <div class="waste-category">
    <i class="icon icon-time-waste"></i>
    <h3>Wasting of Time</h3>
    <p>Time is omnipresent waste with a big difference—not a single second wasted can be recovered.</p>
  </div>
</div>

Industry 5.0 recognizes the rich and growing "on-the-ground-mines" (OTGM)—waste repositories that can deliver valuable resources without capital investment in traditional mining. Carbon Custodians exemplifies this principle by:

<div class="feature-grid">
  <div class="feature-item">
    <i class="icon icon-biomass"></i>
    <h4>Biomass Mining</h4>
    <p>Extracting carbon value from agricultural and forestry waste streams that would otherwise decompose, releasing greenhouse gases.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-item">
    <i class="icon icon-treated"></i>
    <h4>Treated Timber Recovery</h4>
    <p>Safely transforming hazardous treated timber waste into stable carbon products, addressing a significant waste management challenge.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="feature-item">
    <i class="icon icon-contaminated"></i>

- **January 5, 2021**: European Commission releases "Industry 5.0 – Towards a sustainable, human-centric and resilient European industry"
- **September 1, 2021**: Introduction of the Industry 5.0 Waste Prevention Framework
- **September 1, 2022**: Launch of the Industry 5.0 Strategic Alliance
- **March 2024**: United Nations integration of Industry 5.0 in Global Waste Management Outlook
